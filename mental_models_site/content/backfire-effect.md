# Backfire Effect

 **Introduction**

The Backfire Effect is a psychological phenomenon that describes the strengthening of beliefs when presented with evidence contradicting them. This mental model is significant as it underscores the irrationality in human behavior and the challenges we face in persuading people to change their deeply held convictions, especially when these beliefs are linked to personal identity or worldview.

**Core Concept**

Belief perseverance, also known as conceptual conservatism, is a mental model that explains how people tend to maintain their beliefs despite new information that contradicts them. The Backfire Effect is a specific manifestation of this phenomenon where the beliefs become stronger after encountering evidence debunking them. Philosopher F.C.S. Schiller considered belief perseverance as one of the fundamental "laws" of nature due to its consistency with human irrationality.

The Backfire Effect occurs when individuals, in an attempt to maintain psychological consistency and avoid cognitive dissonance, reject or distort information that contradicts their beliefs. They may engage in rationalization (adding new parts to their cognition) or confirmation bias (avoiding contradictory information).

**Examples**

1. **Cults and Religious Beliefs**: One of the earliest examples of belief perseverance can be traced back to a doomsday cult that believed the world would end on December 21, 1954. Despite the non-occurrence of the event, most believers continued to adhere to their faith (Festinger et al., 1956).

2. **Mathematical Errors**: In another example, mathematically capable individuals refused to admit that their assumptions about calculators could have been incorrect, even when presented with contradictory evidence (Ross & Anderson, unspecified year).

3. **Vaccination Misinformation**: More recently, the Backfire Effect has been observed in the context of vaccination misinformation, where providing information on the safety of vaccinations has led to increased vaccination hesitancy (Nyhan et al., 2014).

**Application**

Understanding the Backfire Effect can help individuals recognize when they or others might be engaging in irrational behavior. It encourages critical thinking and open-mindedness, fostering a willingness to reassess beliefs in light of new evidence. In daily life, this means being receptive to different perspectives and avoiding the trap of confirmation bias.

**Related Models**
