# Self-Serving Bias

 Self-Serving Bias: A Cognitive Distortion for Maintaining Self-Esteem
=====================================================================

The mental model of "Self-Serving Bias" is a fundamental cognitive process that can significantly impact our perception and decision-making. It refers to the tendency to perceive oneself in an overly favorable manner, particularly by attributing successes to one's own abilities and efforts while blaming external factors for failures. This bias serves as a protective mechanism for maintaining and enhancing self-esteem.

**Core Concept**

The Self-Serving Bias operates through the interplay of motivational processes such as self-enhancement and self-presentation, and cognitive processes like locus of control and self-esteem. Individuals with a high need for self-esteem often exhibit this bias, attributing successes internally and failures externally to maintain their favorable self-image.

**Examples**

1. **Business**: A CEO might attribute the company's success to their strategic decisions and leadership skills, while blaming external factors like market conditions or competitor actions for any losses.
2. **Life**: An individual may excel in a sports event due to their skill and hard work, but blame the referee or equipment for a loss, maintaining their positive self-perception.
3. **History**: During the 1960s space race, astronauts like Neil Armstrong were hailed as heroes for their achievements, while failures in missions were often attributed to technical issues or external factors.

**Application**

Understanding the Self-Serving Bias can help individuals become more aware of their biases and make more objective decisions. By recognizing when they are using this bias, people can work towards being more accountable for their failures and fairer in acknowledging others' contributions to successes.

**Related Models**

1. **Confirmation Bias**: The tendency to seek out information that confirms our existing beliefs while ignoring or dismissing contradictory evidence.
2. **Cognitive Dissonance Theory**: A theory suggesting that individuals strive for internal consistency and will change their behavior, attitudes, or beliefs to reduce cognitive dissonance, which is the mental discomfort experienced when holding two or more inconsistent thoughts, beliefs, or values at the same time.

In conclusion, the Self-Serving Bias is a powerful mental model that significantly impacts our perceptions and decisions. By understanding this bias, we can strive to make more objective assessments of ourselves and others, fostering a healthier self-esteem and better decision-making skills.
